1. `CRITICAL` Cross-phase comparison validity  
Phase 2 is from April 8, 2026 and Phase 3 from April 12, 2026; the raw logs show `ollama_version=0.20.2` in the Phase 2 subset and `0.20.6` in Phase 3. The logs do not preserve a usable model digest either: `model_digest` is blank throughout both runs, so exact model-build parity is unverifiable. The comparison also mixes `390` records/model/condition in Phase 2 (`10` reps, seeds `1-10`) with `312` in Phase 3 (`8` reps, seeds `1-8`). The analysis only aligns on question IDs [analyze_mechanism.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/analysis/analyze_mechanism.py:716), yet the report says that makes the comparison “valid” [analyze_mechanism.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/analysis/analyze_mechanism.py:429). That caveat should be stronger: these are historical controls on the same items, not same-run counterfactuals. As a sensitivity check, the first `8` Phase 2 reps already move some references: Gemma `C` is `61.5%` on all `10` reps but `59.9%` on the matched first `8`; Qwen `C` is `50.3%` vs `51.9%`.

2. `CRITICAL` Qwen L-condition truncation confound  
I do not see evidence that `L25/L50/L75/L100` accidentally turned thinking on. `L` is explicitly `think=False, prefill=True` [condition_builder.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/runner/condition_builder.py:196), but every prefill condition gets `num_predict=8192` in dispatch [run_experiment.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/runner/run_experiment.py:167). In the raw Phase 3 log, Qwen `L25/L50/L75/L100` have `121/143/142/143` ceiling hits, and those exact counts also appear as `done_reason="length"`. So these are ordinary long raw continuations, not hidden-think outputs. The likely mechanism is that the assistant-prefill trace primes Qwen to keep extending the reasoning in the assistant turn; sample prompts start with things like `Thinking Process:` and the model keeps going until the `8192` cap. This makes the Qwen dose-response largely uninterpretable as a trace-dose effect: within Qwen, ceiling-hit ITT is only `40.5/35.0/31.0/41.3%` for `L25/L50/L75/L100`, versus `78.5/72.2/68.2/60.9%` when no ceiling hit occurs.

3. `CRITICAL` M vs L100 confound: trace source quality  
`M` is not just “compressed self-trace.” `L100` uses the model’s raw B-trace [condition_builder.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/runner/condition_builder.py:196), but `M` pulls `q_id_compressed` scaffolds from the external scaffold store [run_experiment.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/runner/run_experiment.py:144), i.e. Sonnet-authored compressed traces, then strips the final answer [condition_builder.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/runner/condition_builder.py:210). So `M vs L100` changes both length and author/editor quality. The reported gaps, Gemma `73.4% vs 61.2%` and Qwen `88.8% vs 51.9%`, are consistent with “stronger trace author plus shorter/cleaner prompt” as much as “compression helps.” To disentangle this, you need same-source comparisons: raw B-trace vs compressed B-trace from the same original trace, and raw Sonnet scaffold vs compressed Sonnet scaffold.

4. `IMPORTANT` K-C “Simpson’s paradox” interpretation  
This is not Simpson’s paradox. The model-specific effects already disagree in sign, so this is interaction/heterogeneity, not an aggregate reversal of same-sign subgroup effects. The asymmetry is very plausibly trace-verbosity mismatch. Qwen `K` receives Gemma traces and has mean `prompt_eval_count=881.4`, versus Qwen `C` at `3615.8`; Gemma `K` receives Qwen traces and has mean `prompt_eval_count=3658.3`, versus Gemma `C` at `881.4`. That lines up with the outcomes: Qwen improves from `50.3%` to `67.9%` and ceiling drops from `46%` to `16%`, while Gemma falls from `61.5%` to `50.3%` and extraction failure rises from `2%` to `14%`. So “cross-model compatibility” is overclaimed; “donor trace length/style matters” is at least as good an explanation.

5. `IMPORTANT` Dose-response statistical power  
The aggregate trend analysis is too weak to carry interpretive weight. The code claims “Jonckheere-Terpstra” in the header, but actually computes Kendall’s tau on only `4` aggregate dose points plus a `4`-point weighted logit [analyze_mechanism.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/analysis/analyze_mechanism.py:181). With `n=4`, even perfect monotonicity barely reaches `p=0.0833`; your reported `tau=±0.667, p=0.3333` is therefore basically non-informative. The primary analysis should be trial-level, not 4-point aggregate: logistic mixed model or GEE on all `1248` L-observations per model, with numeric dose, question random intercepts, and a formal `model × dose` interaction. A paired within-question/per-rep permutation test would also be stronger than the current tau summary.

6. `IMPORTANT` Gemma `L25 < A`  
Treat this as suggestive, not robust. The reported result, `50.0% vs 59.7%, p_adj=0.040`, is a cross-phase historical-control contrast on a subset selected using Phase 2 A informativeness, so it is vulnerable to both selection and date/version drift. That said, it is not obviously just a rep-count artifact: on the matched first `8` Phase 2 reps, Gemma `A` is `60.6%`, so the gap remains `-10.6pp`. And within Phase 3, Gemma `L100=61.2%` while `L25=50.0%`, `L50=48.7%`, and `L75=52.9%`, which does support “short partial self-traces underperform fuller traces.” What is not yet cleanly established is the stronger claim “quarter trace is worse than no context.”

7. `CRITICAL` Qwen N-B comparison  
For Qwen, the `N-B` contrast is largely uninformative as stated. On the 39-item subset, `B` has `242/390` ceiling hits and `241/390` extraction failures; `N` has `184/312` ceiling hits and `200/312` extraction failures. So the ITT tie at `23.1% vs 23.1%` mostly says both configurations often run into the cap and fail. The PP comparison, `64.3% vs 60.4%`, is a survivor analysis conditioned on a post-treatment variable; it does not cleanly answer whether greedy vs stochastic thinking helps. The report should say explicitly that, under the `8192` cap, Qwen `N-B` does not provide an interpretable test of deterministic thinking.
