**Kill Shot.**
The paper’s central Qwen claim is not established by the confirmatory experiment; it is manufactured by a selective, post-treatment repair of one arm and then elevated into the headline. The abstract, Table 2, §4 Claim 3, and §8 all lean on the 16K-merged result that Qwen `B−C` is null ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:8), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:125), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:213), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:268)). But §2.7/§3.4/§6 admit that only 367 ceiling-hit Qwen-B cases were rerun, chosen on a post-treatment outcome, and the manifest says those replacements were collected under a different Ollama version while per-inference digests and generation options were never logged ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:91), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:176), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:243), [RUN_MANIFEST.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/RUN_MANIFEST.md:69), [RUN_MANIFEST.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/RUN_MANIFEST.md:77)). That is not a clean counterfactual and it does not support the paper’s main mechanistic conclusion.

**Critical Flaws**
- `CRITICAL:` §4 Claim 3, “Hidden thinking and trace replay are statistically indistinguishable for Qwen at a doubled budget,” is not supported by a symmetric or confirmatory comparison. Only Qwen `B` was rerun; `C` was not, yet Table 1 still shows Qwen `C` with 30% in the `Ceil` column and Qwen `B` with 20% extraction failure even after repair ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:103), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:213)). A full-cohort rerun of both arms at the same budget is the minimum required before claiming the original reversal was a truncation artifact.
- `CRITICAL:` The strongest headline result, §4 Claim 1 “Semantic content of reasoning is essential,” relies on a control that the paper itself says is mechanically invalid. §7 states that the shuffle used for `F` may fragment-decode at BPE boundaries, and that the token-matching field was off by ~1.79× for `G` and `J` ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:261)). Table 1 then shows the exact failure signature of a broken control: `F` has extraction failure 34%/57% and `J` has 40% extraction failure for Qwen. Table 3’s huge Part-1 ORs confirm that `C−F` and `C−J` are partly measuring malformed prompt behavior, not just semantic content ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:157), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:211)).
- `CRITICAL:` The reproducibility claim fails on provenance. The preregistration promises per-inference model digest and full generation parameters, and even says fixed seeds enable “exact reproduction” ([osf-preregistration.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/outputs/osf-preregistration.md:121), [osf-preregistration.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/outputs/osf-preregistration.md:249)). RUN_MANIFEST says the digest field is blank in all logs and the `options` dict was never logged, only reconstructed later ([RUN_MANIFEST.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/RUN_MANIFEST.md:77)). For a paper about inference-stack-sensitive behavior, that is publication-blocking.
- `CRITICAL:` The paper repeatedly says the rerun held “all other settings” and the “same model build” fixed (§2.7, §3.4), but the manifest directly undercuts that: Run 3 used Ollama 0.20.6, Run 1 used 0.20.2, and model digests were not logged at collection time ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:93), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:178), [RUN_MANIFEST.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/RUN_MANIFEST.md:65)). The central sensitivity analysis is therefore not even verifiably same-stack.

**Important Flaws**
- `IMPORTANT:` The statistical plan drift is material. The preregistered primary model was Bayesian hierarchical logistic with task-type terms and a preregistered TOST equivalence test for `B−C` ([osf-preregistration.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/outputs/osf-preregistration.md:171), [osf-preregistration.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/outputs/osf-preregistration.md:194)). The paper swaps to per-model GEE, drops task-type from the primary analysis, and replaces equivalence with “statistically indistinguishable” (§2.6, §6, §4.3). For a null headline, that is not acceptable without a full preregistered-model sensitivity.
- `IMPORTANT:` The mechanism claim is overstated. Gemma still shows a significant live-thinking premium (`B−C` OR=1.53, p_adj=.003, Table 2), and Qwen conditional-on-extraction also favors live thinking (Table 3 Part 2 OR=2.17, p<.001). The data support “trace replay recovers much of capped E2E performance,” not the stronger conclusion in §8 that most benefit is trace-carried.
- `IMPORTANT:` The calibration story is bad. §2.2 says items were locked in a 30–70% baseline band, but realized baseline accuracy is 82.8%/82.6%. That is not minor drift; it means the locked set is near-ceiling for `A`, undermining the stated rationale for question selection.
- `IMPORTANT:` Temperature 0.7 is a major confound, not a footnote. §7 concedes Qwen runaway may be sampling-induced. In a paper whose key phenomenon is verbosity/truncation, not running the primary comparison at T=0 or a temperature sweep is a serious design omission.

**Minor Flaws**
- `MINOR:` The rerun count is inconsistent: abstract says 368, §2.7/§3.4 and the manifest say 367, while the merge script header says 368 ([paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:8), [paper.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/paper/paper.md:93), [RUN_MANIFEST.md](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/RUN_MANIFEST.md:45), [merge_16k_rerun.py](/home/james-fowler/github/Workflows/workflows/thinking-token-experiment/scripts/merge_16k_rerun.py:7)).
- `MINOR:` Table 1’s `Ceil` label is misleading in the merged dataset; the analysis code defines `ceiling_hit` as `eval_count >= 8190`, so it is not the current-cap ceiling after the 16K rerun.
- `MINOR:` Terminology drifts: the paper says GEE is “not hierarchical,” but artifacts are still labeled “Hierarchical GEE” in the manifest.

**What The Authors Do Right.**
The paper is much more candid than usual. It explicitly retracts earlier overclaims, separates E2E from conditional-on-extraction, and admits several serious limitations rather than burying them. The within-model framing is also the right instinct.

**Recommendation.**
Reject. The headline result rests on a selectively repaired, unverifiable sensitivity analysis, and several core confirmatory contrasts rely on controls the paper itself admits are not protocol-faithful.
