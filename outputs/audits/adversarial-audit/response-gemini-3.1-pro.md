**1. Kill shot**

The paper’s headline claim that hidden thinking and trace replay are "statistically indistinguishable" for Qwen (Claim 3) is built on a fatal, publication-blocking methodological error. In §3.4 and §2.7, the authors disclose a 16K "sensitivity rerun" where they doubled the generation budget *only* for the 367 Qwen Condition B inferences that hit the 8K ceiling. They did not re-run the 662 Qwen B inferences that succeeded at 8K, nor did they re-run Condition C (which suffered a 30% ceiling hit rate at 8K according to Table 1), nor Conditions F, G, or J. They then merged this selectively rescued Qwen B data with the 8K data for the other conditions and used this frankenstein dataset for their primary GEE contrasts in Table 2. You cannot systematically intervene to rescue failures in the treatment arm (B) while leaving the control arm (C) to suffer its 30% truncation rate at the original budget, and then claim the two are "statistically indistinguishable." The B−C contrast (log-OR = -0.009) on the "merged" dataset is a mathematically invalid comparison of a partial-16K budget against a strict-8K budget. 

**2. Critical flaws**

*   **CRITICAL: Causal claims based on post-treatment collider bias.** In §3.3, the authors use a two-part hurdle model to claim that Qwen B "improves reasoning conditional on extraction (OR=2.17, p<.001)." Extraction failure is a post-treatment variable heavily influenced by the condition (as they explicitly note: Qwen B ExtF OR=5.17). Conditioning an analysis on a post-treatment collider introduces severe selection bias. The subset of questions where Qwen B successfully extracts a final answer is systematically different (e.g., shorter intrinsic reasoning paths, simpler logic) than the subset where Condition C extracts. Comparing their accuracy conditional on this endogenous selection violates the fundamental assumptions of causal inference.
*   **CRITICAL: Irremediable provenance gaps and version mixing.** The `RUN_MANIFEST.md` admits that `model_digest` and generation options were not logged per-inference. Worse, it explicitly documents that Phase 2 was run on Ollama 0.20.2, while the Phase 3 and the 16K Qwen B reruns were run on Ollama 0.20.6. The primary "merged" dataset thus contains unrecorded version heterogeneity *within a single condition* (662 Qwen B records at 0.20.2, 367 at 0.20.6). You cannot claim reproducible science when the inference engine changes mid-condition and the exact weights loaded into memory cannot be cryptographically verified from the logs. 
*   **CRITICAL: Invalid GEE application under complete separation.** In §3.3, Table 3 reports Part 1 (Extraction Failure) ORs for Gemma of "→0" or "→∞". This occurs because Conditions D, I, and O have exactly 0% extraction failure. Applying Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) with a logit link when there is zero variance in the outcome for specific clusters/treatments violates the regularity conditions required for the robust sandwich estimator. The variance-covariance matrix in these separated cells is degenerate, rendering the standard errors—and thus the Holm-Bonferroni adjusted p-values in Table 2 for any contrast involving D, I, or O—mathematically meaningless.

**3. Important flaws**

*   **IMPORTANT: Falsified "Token-Matched" claims.** Claim 5 (§4) asserts "Coherent reasoning beats same-length filler" based on the C−J contrast. However, §7 (Limitations) admits that the trace bank used for Conditions G and J relied on a legacy word-based count that differs from actual BPE token counts by ~1.79×. The lengths are not matched. If the filler in J is nearly twice as long as the reasoning in C, the performance drop could be entirely driven by context-window dilution or attention degradation, completely confounding the "semantic content" claim.
*   **IMPORTANT: Misleading justification for pre-registration deviation.** In §2.6, the authors justify abandoning their pre-registered Bayesian hierarchical model in favor of frequentist GEE by claiming they require a "population-averaged (marginal) estimand." Yet, their secondary hurdle model (§3.3) and trial-level dose-response analysis (§3.5) rely heavily on conditional estimands. You cannot invoke the necessity of marginal estimands to dodge a pre-registered Bayesian protocol while selectively leaning on conditional estimands when it suits the narrative (e.g., claiming Qwen B beats C on reasoning quality).
*   **IMPORTANT: Task simplicity masks true capability.** §2.2 admits the calibration target was 30–70%, but realized baseline (Condition A) accuracy was ~83%. The tasks are too easy. Operating this close to the ceiling severely compresses the dynamic range available to detect the benefits of thinking tokens, strongly biasing all baseline-comparative contrasts (like B-A or E-A) toward the null. 

**4. Minor flaws**

*   **MINOR: False precision in reporting.** Table 1 reports accuracy and proportions to three decimal places (e.g., 0.828) based on cell sizes of ~1,000. This implies a level of precision that is statistically unsupported by the sample size.
*   **MINOR: Unsubstantiated "Expert Premium" framing.** Calling D−C an "expert scaffold" effect (§4, Claim 2) is highly presumptuous. Condition D uses GPT-5.4 text. If the smaller model fails to utilize it, it is equally likely an out-of-distribution formatting penalty or a vocabulary mismatch rather than a profound insight into how models utilize "expert" reasoning.

**5. What the authors do right**

The experimental design's core ambition—systematically dissociating the mechanical act of generating thinking tokens from their semantic content, length, and shape via Conditions B through J—is genuinely excellent. Furthermore, acknowledging that standard end-to-end accuracy metrics conflate formatting/extraction failures with actual reasoning failures (and attempting to separate them via the hurdle model, despite its execution flaws) demonstrates a sophisticated understanding of LLM evaluation pathologies that most papers completely ignore. 

**6. Recommendation**

**Reject.** The paper constructs its primary headline claims on a mathematically invalid dataset that merges an un-replicated 8K baseline with a selectively-rescued 16K treatment arm, entirely invalidating the core B vs C contrast.
